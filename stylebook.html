The Sacramento Daze Stylebook

Rules of capitalization, naming, numbering, punctuation


ACRONYMS

When an acronym shortens full words ["Golden State Warriors"] periods separate its letters ["G.S.W."]. When an acronym shortens one word ["Miami"] no periods are used ["MIA"].

When listing a statistic, however, no periods are used ["Field Goal Attempt" = "FGA"].

In most all cases, an acronym is never used without first using its full words.

BRACKETS

A purely aesthetic choice, instead of parentheses.

DASHES & HYPHENS

Dashes appear as two hyphens ["--"] and either mark an interlude in a sentence ["...this is a good time to recognize -- given the politics of most N.B.A. owners/governors -- the relative decency of the Kings'..."] or separate a sentence fragment ["...writing civil rights violations into law--mainly in the form of..."].

Hyphens ["-"] are used conventionally ["21st-most" or "A-Rod"] as well as to improve readability ["under-represented"].

ELLIPSES

These are used to either mark ommitted text ["We shall go on to the end, we shall fight in France,... we shall fight on the beaches,..."] or for effect ["wait a second..."].

NUMBERING

Numbers zero through nine are spelled out, except when there's a decimal ["2.4" or "6.8"]. Numbers 10 and above are shown as numbers.

When listing a statistic or measurement, however, the number appears as a number, even if it's below 10 ["7 points per game" or "6'4""].

When a number starts a sentence it is always spelled out ["Fifty-five days ago, I..."].

OXFORD COMMA

Yes.

PLAYER NAMES

At first mention, a player's full name is used ["Harrison Barnes" or "Chris Paul"]. For subsequent mentions, if that player is on the Kings, only his first name is used ["Harrison"]; if that player is not on the Kings, only his last name is used ["Paul"].

Nicknames of players ["The Black Falcon" or "CP3"], both on the Kings and not, may be used as well.

SHORT-HAND

Reductions of full words to fragments ["Points" to "Pts."] include a period, except when a single letter stands for an entire word ["owner/governor" to "o/g"].
